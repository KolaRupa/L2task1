"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

About the Legend

  • Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a renowned Indian scientist, statesman, and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and played a crucial role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs.

  • Dr. Kalam was also known as the "Missile Man of India" for his significant contributions to the field of aerospace engineering. He was instrumental in the successful development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, and the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

  • Aside from his scientific achievements, Dr. Abdul Kalam was a great visionary and an inspiration to millions of people, especially the youth. He emphasized the importance of education, hard work, and perseverance. He believed in the power of dreams and encouraged young minds to dream big and work tirelessly to achieve their goals.

  • Dr. Kalam served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was widely respected for his humility, integrity, and dedication to the welfare of the nation. Even after his presidency, he continued to engage with students and young people, delivering motivational speeches and sharing his wisdom.

  • Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam left an indelible mark on the world through his scientific achievements, leadership, and vision for a better and prosperous India. He passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy and teachings continue to inspire generations.
